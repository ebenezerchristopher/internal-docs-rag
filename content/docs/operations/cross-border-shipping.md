# Cross-Border Shipping

## Documentation
Every cross-border shipment must have: commercial invoice, packing list,
bill of lading, and any required certificates (origin, phytosanitary,
fumigation). The customs broker is responsible for filing; Ops is
responsible for providing accurate commodity descriptions and values.

## Incoterms
Default to DAP for most clients. Use DDP only when the client has explicitly
asked us to handle duties and taxes; in that case, estimate duties at quote
time and reconcile on the actual invoice.

## Common holds
- ISF (10+2): must be filed at least 24 hours before vessel loading for US
  ocean imports. Missing this window is a $5,000 fine per shipment.
- ACE/ACI: US and Canada entry filings. Same-day filing is required.
- Wood packaging: must be ISPM-15 stamped for all modes.

## Escalation
Customs holds escalate to the broker first, then to the carrier, then to
the consignee. Ops does not negotiate with customs directly.
