# Delay Complaint SOP

## When this applies
Use this SOP whenever a client reports that a shipment is late, regardless of
whether the delay is on us, the carrier, customs, or weather. The goal is a
single fast response that includes a new ETA, an explanation, and a follow-up
commitment.

## Steps

### 1. Acknowledge within 1 business hour
Reply from the shared inbox using the "delay-ack" template. Include the order
ID, the original promise date, and a placeholder for the new ETA. Do not promise
a specific time until step 3.

### 2. Pull the live status
Check the TMS for the most recent scan. If the last scan is older than 24
hours, escalate to Ops on-call immediately; do not wait for the client to ask
again.

### 3. Set a new ETA
Use the carrier's published transit time for the remaining leg. Add a 1-day
buffer unless the client has explicitly accepted an unbuffered ETA in writing.

### 4. Offer the standard remedy
- 0-2 day delay: written apology, no credit.
- 3-7 day delay: 5% credit, written apology, dedicated point of contact.
- 8+ day delay: 15% credit, written apology, dedicated point of contact,
  post-incident review with the carrier.

### 5. Follow up the day of delivery
Send a one-line "did this arrive as expected?" check. If the answer is no,
open a new ticket and switch to the Damaged Freight SOP.

## What not to do
- Do not blame the carrier in writing without Legal review.
- Do not promise a refund larger than the standard remedy without Director
  approval.
- Do not close the ticket until the client has confirmed receipt.
