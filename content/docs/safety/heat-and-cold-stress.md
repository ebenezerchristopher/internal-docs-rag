# Heat and Cold Stress

## Heat
- Water is available at every active work area.
- Mandatory 10-minute cool-down every 2 hours when the heat index
  exceeds 95F.
- Work is suspended when the heat index exceeds 105F for non-acclimated
  workers.

## Cold
- Layered clothing is encouraged; the company provides insulated
  gloves, hats, and base layers as needed.
- Mandatory 10-minute warm-up every 2 hours when the wind chill
  drops below 0F.
- Work is suspended when the wind chill drops below -25F.

## Recognition
- Heat exhaustion: heavy sweating, weakness, clammy skin.
- Heat stroke: hot dry skin, confusion, loss of consciousness.
- Frostbite: numbness, white or grayish-yellow skin.
- Hypothermia: shivering, slurred speech, loss of coordination.

## Response
Any suspected heat or cold injury is a medical emergency. Call 911,
move the worker to a temperate area, and apply first aid until help
arrives.
